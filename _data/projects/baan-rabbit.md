description: |
  A home for a running-loving couple, their father, and five rabbits. This project is a renovation of a 30-year-old townhouse with 3 bedrooms and 3 bathrooms.

  The original house faced several challenges: low ceiling heights, limited openings, and poor ventilation, resulting in a dark, enclosed, and uncomfortable interior.

  One of the key requirements was the integration of a solar cell system. The design introduces a tilted facade, optimized for maximum sun exposure while providing efficient space for solar panel installation.

  The core design approach focuses on "reopening" the house — bringing back light, air, and spatial clarity. A small front courtyard is introduced to improve airflow. A double-space acts as a thermal buffer, reducing heat gain. The living area is reconfigured into an open plan, enhancing openness and connection.

  The spatial organization is based on the existing structural grid: 3-meter grid for service areas (kitchen, bathrooms, bedrooms), 4-meter grid for open living spaces for relaxation and shared activities. The result is a home that feels light, breathable, and alive — designed for both human living and the comfort of their pets.
